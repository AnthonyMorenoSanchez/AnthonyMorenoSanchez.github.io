Courses

  • Neuromatch Academy — Computational Neuroscience

    The Neuromatch Academy – Computational Neuroscience course provides a rigorous, hands-on education in computational neuroscience that is entirely remote. Over three weeks, students work in small pods on a team-based project where they define scientific questions, analyze real neural or behavioral data, and iteratively refine hypotheses through modeling. This course provides the ability to work with a variety of students and professionals from different disciples, highlighting a variety of concepts, and approaches to neuroscience research.

    Key high-level concepts covered include:

    • Modeling & Dynamical Systems — build interpretable models that reflect biologically plausible neural dynamics.
    • Statistical & Machine Learning Methods — use generalized linear models, dimensionality reduction (e.g., PCA), clustering, and deep learning to extract structure from data.
    • Stochastic Processes & Reinforcement Learning — explore hidden dynamics, optimal control, and decision-making under uncertainty.
    • Causality vs. Correlation — distinguish causal relationships from mere correlations in neural systems.
    • Scientific Practice & Iterative Research — learn how to design a research question, run exploratory analyses, write a hypothesis-driven abstract, and adapt your approach based on feedback.
  • Simons Computational Neuroscience IMBIZO — Class of 2024

    Funded through the International Brain Research Organization (IBRO) and Simons foundation the IMBIZO hosted in person in South Africa, brings together researchers across Africa for three weeks to collaborate on AI and data science initiatives. IMBIZO Each individual week includes lectures and hands on experience in different computational methods building a deep understanding of neuroscience and AI.

    • Cycle 1: Molecules to Behavior — Introduction to how biophysical mechanisms in single cells form the foundation of neural networks, with a focus on analyzing structure, function, and specialized architectures that support behavior.
    • Cycle 2: Cells to circuits to behavior — Exploring how brain biology and dynamics give rise to systems-level functions like sensory processing, birdsong, motor control, and decision-making.
    • Cycle 3: NeuroAI — Examining the shared principles of biological and artificial intelligence, highlighting how biological principles can inform machine learning and how machine learning can help understand the brain.

Courses

  • Neuromatch Academy Computational Neuroscience

    The Neuromatch Academy – Computational Neuroscience course provides a rigorous, hands-on education in computational neuroscience, designed to mirror real research experience. Over three weeks, students work in small pods on a team-based project where they define scientific questions, analyze real neural or behavioral data, and iteratively refine hypotheses through modeling.

    Key high-level concepts covered include:

    • Modeling & Dynamical Systems — build interpretable models that reflect biologically plausible neural dynamics.
    • Statistical & Machine Learning Methods — use generalized linear models, dimensionality reduction (e.g., PCA), clustering, and deep learning to extract structure from data.
    • Stochastic Processes & Reinforcement Learning — explore hidden dynamics, optimal control, and decision-making under uncertainty.
    • Causality vs. Correlation — distinguish causal relationships from mere correlations in neural systems.
    • Scientific Practice & Iterative Research — learn how to design a research question, run exploratory analyses, write a hypothesis-driven abstract, and adapt your approach based on feedback.

    For recruiters, this training demonstrates your ability to:

    • Work independently and collaboratively in a research-style team, communicating ideas and sharing code.
    • Design and execute a data-driven project, including literature review, hypothesis formation, analysis, and presentation.
    • Apply advanced quantitative tools (machine learning, dimensionality reduction, modeling) to real neuroscience datasets.
    • Think about high-level computational neuroscience problems such as sparsity, mixed-selectivity, predictive coding, and generalization.

    Completing this course signals strong preparation for roles in academia (e.g., graduate research, computational neuroscience labs) or industry (machine learning, data science, neurotechnology), highlighting both your technical skills and your experience in a real research workflow.
